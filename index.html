Escape from Kirtland

A Restoration History Experience — BYU REL C 225

January 12, 1838. Kirtland, Ohio. Midnight.

You are a faithful member of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. The community you once called home is fracturing. The Kirtland Safety Society has collapsed amid a national economic panic. Trusted leaders have turned against the Prophet. A dissenter faction has seized the Kirtland Temple. Mobs roam the streets.

Tonight, Joseph Smith and Sidney Rigdon fled on horseback to escape arrest and mob violence. You must follow — but the road out of Kirtland is not easy. To safely join the Saints gathering in Missouri, you must prove you understand what happened here, and why faithful disciples chose to follow their Prophet into the unknown.

— ✦ —

To Escape Kirtland, You Must Complete Four Challenges:

🔑 Challenge 1 — The Restoration Timeline
Arrange the key events of the Restoration — from the First Vision to the Kirtland Temple — in correct chronological order.
🔑 Challenge 2 — Evaluating the Sources
Identify which historical sources about the Restoration are reliable, and why.
🔑 Challenge 3 — Revelation Matching
Match Doctrine and Covenants revelations to their Kirtland-era historical context.
🔑 Challenge 4 — Modern Application
Connect the principles of the Restoration to the living Church today.
— ✦ —

This experience connects to the four outcomes of REL C 225:
Factual  ·  Conceptual  ·  Spiritual  ·  Application

Based on Saints: The Standard of Truth, the Joseph Smith Papers, Church History Topics (ChurchofJesusChrist.org), and the BYU Religious Studies Center

You Have Escaped Kirtland!

You Join the Saints on the Road to Missouri

With the full story of the Restoration alive in your heart, you slip out of Kirtland under cover of darkness — following the path of your Prophet westward. You carry with you the truths that began in a grove of trees in 1820: a living God who speaks, a restored priesthood, a second testament of Jesus Christ, and a Church organized by revelation.

No mob, no economic crisis, no apostate, and no persecution could take those things from you. The Restoration — from the First Vision to the Kirtland Temple and beyond — had established a foundation that would outlast every trial. The road to Missouri was hard. But the truth you carried was worth every step.

What You Learned: The Four Course Outcomes

📚

Factual

You demonstrated knowledge of the major events of the Restoration — the First Vision (1820), Moroni's visits, receiving the plates, the restoration of the Aaronic and Melchizedek Priesthoods, the Book of Mormon's publication, the organization of the Church (1830), and the Kirtland Temple dedication (1836).

🧠

Conceptual

You practiced evaluating historical sources using critical thinking — distinguishing primary accounts from secondary sources, recognizing bias, and identifying the difference between peer-reviewed scholarship and unsourced claims. These are essential skills for responsible Church history study.

✨

Spiritual

You connected key Doctrine and Covenants revelations — D&C 13, 76, 88, 89, and 110 — to their Kirtland-era historical contexts, deepening your appreciation for how the Lord restored His gospel "line upon line, precept upon precept" in this remarkable period.

🌍

Application

You connected the founding principles of the Restoration — the nature of God, priesthood authority, continuing revelation, the gathering of Israel, and the Book of Mormon — to the living Church of Jesus Christ today, showing that what began in 1820 is still unfolding.
